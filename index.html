Inspection

Details of the pilot inspection are sumarized below.

1. Hardware Integration

Abyss Solutions integrated its high-fidelity optical imaging system on Proceanic's ROV. The system consisted of a stereo imaging set as well as specialized lighting. The system was capable of capturing high detail and accurate colour imagery as well as precise 3D measurements.

2. ROV Inspection

A 3D printed replica of part of a mooring chain was inspected using the ROV within a test tank provided by Exxonmobil. The ROV was navigated along the length of the chain to capture imagery from multiple perspectives. Following claibration and testing, 3 chain links were inspected within 1 hour.

3. Onsite Analysis

A subset of the imagery was used to generate preliminary 3D models of the chains onsite. Approximate measurements of the chain were provided to onsite staff within 15 minutes of data capture. The provision of measurements was interactive with staff able to nominate the precise measurements required.

Analysis

Details of the analysis approach adopted are provided below.

1. Image Enhancement

Imagery collected was subject to image enhancement to improve the appearance of the fine details of the chain links and the representation of chain geomtry. 

2. Photorealistic 3D Models

The enahnced imagery was used to re-construct a true colour and high-detail virtual replica of the chain links. The models were produced to scale for the purpose of 3D measurement. 

3. Dimensional measurement

The 3D models were used to estimate bar diameters in the plane (a) and orthogonal (b) to the face of each link. Additionally grip lengths (c) were determined for each end of the center link.
